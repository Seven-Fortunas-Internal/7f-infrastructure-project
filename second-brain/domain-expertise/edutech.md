# Educational Technology (EdTech)

## Learning Science and Pedagogy

### Core Learning Principles
- **Active Learning**: Learners construct knowledge through engagement
- **Scaffolding**: Providing support that decreases as competence increases
- **Metacognition**: Helping learners understand their own learning processes
- **Transfer**: Applying learned concepts to new contexts
- **Motivation**: Intrinsic and extrinsic factors driving learning

### Learning Theories
- **Constructivism**: Learners build understanding through experience
- **Behaviorism**: Learning driven by stimulus-response and reinforcement
- **Cognitive Load Theory**: Capacity limits in working memory
- **Bloom's Taxonomy**: Hierarchy of learning objectives (remember → create)
- **Growth Mindset**: Belief that abilities can be developed through effort

### Instructional Design Approaches
- **ADDIE**: Analysis, Design, Development, Implementation, Evaluation
- **Backward Design**: Start with desired learning outcomes
- **Spaced Repetition**: Spreading practice over time
- **Interleaving**: Mixing different types of problems/content
- **Retrieval Practice**: Testing to strengthen memory

## EdTech Platform Architectures

### Learning Management Systems (LMS)
- **Core Features**: Course content, assignments, grading, communication
- **Platforms**: Blackboard, Canvas, Moodle, Brightspace, Schoology
- **Institutional Use**: Most common in K-12 and higher education
- **Admin Burden**: Content management, enrollment, gradebook management
- **Integration**: API access to external tools (standards like LTI)

### Adaptive Learning Platforms
- **Personalization**: Content adjusts based on learner performance
- **Diagnostics**: Identify knowledge gaps and learning needs
- **Recommendations**: Suggest next learning activities
- **Technologies**: AI/ML for performance prediction and adaptation
- **Examples**: Knewton, ALEKS, DreamBox, 3-Brain

### Synchronous Learning Tools
- **Video Conferencing**: Zoom, Google Meet, Microsoft Teams
- **Virtual Classrooms**: Interactive whiteboard and collaboration
- **Real-time Interaction**: Chat, polls, breakout rooms
- **Recording**: Capture sessions for asynchronous access
- **Accessibility**: Screen sharing, closed captions, accessible UI

### Assessment and Analytics Tools
- **Formative Assessment**: Quizzes, surveys during learning
- **Summative Assessment**: Final exams and projects
- **Data Analytics**: Dashboards showing learner progress
- **Predictive Analytics**: Identifying at-risk learners
- **Standards Mapping**: Alignment to learning standards

## Student Engagement and Retention

### Engagement Strategies
- **Gamification**: Points, badges, leaderboards to motivate
- **Community Building**: Peer interaction and collaboration features
- **Personalization**: Customized learning paths and recommendations
- **Feedback**: Timely, actionable feedback on performance
- **Relevance**: Content connected to real-world applications

### Retention Metrics
- **Completion Rate**: Percentage of enrolled students finishing course
- **Time-to-Completion**: How long courses actually take
- **Time-on-Task**: Engagement with course materials
- **Dropout Rate**: When students typically disengage
- **Churn Analysis**: Understanding why students leave

### Intervention Strategies
- **Early Warning Systems**: Predictive models identifying at-risk students
- **Proactive Outreach**: Communication from instructors/advisors
- **Support Services**: Tutoring, counseling, academic resources
- **Flexible Pacing**: Allowing self-paced and extended timelines
- **Payment Plans**: Making education more financially accessible

## Curriculum Design and Content Management

### Curriculum Development Process
1. **Needs Assessment**: Understand target learner needs
2. **Goal Setting**: Define learning outcomes
3. **Content Selection**: Identify key concepts and skills
4. **Sequencing**: Determine optimal order of content
5. **Instructional Design**: Choose delivery methods
6. **Evaluation**: Assess effectiveness and iterate

### Learning Outcomes (Learning Objectives)
- **SMART Criteria**: Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound
- **Bloom's Levels**: Remember → Understand → Apply → Analyze → Evaluate → Create
- **Granularity**: Course-level → Module-level → Lesson-level outcomes
- **Assessment Alignment**: Outcomes should drive assessment design

### Content Formats
- **Text and Reading**: Textbooks, articles, handouts
- **Video**: Lectures, demonstrations, animations
- **Interactive**: Simulations, labs, practice problems
- **Multimedia**: Combining multiple content types
- **Microlearning**: Short, focused learning units

### Content Management Systems (CMS) for Education
- **Repositories**: Storing and organizing learning objects
- **Versioning**: Tracking content updates
- **Accessibility**: Meeting accessibility standards (WCAG)
- **IP Protection**: Managing intellectual property rights
- **Reusability**: Packaging content for reuse

## Assessment and Testing Frameworks

### Assessment Types
- **Diagnostic**: Assessing prior knowledge and gaps
- **Formative**: Ongoing assessment during learning
- **Summative**: Final assessment of learning outcomes
- **Standardized**: Nationally/internationally normed tests
- **Authentic**: Real-world problem solving and applications

### Testing and Evaluation
- **Reliability**: Consistent measurement of learning
- **Validity**: Actually measuring what we intend to measure
- **Item Analysis**: Understanding difficulty and discrimination of questions
- **Rubrics**: Detailed scoring guides for subjective assessments
- **Cheating Prevention**: Academic integrity in online exams

### Grading and Reporting
- **Grading Scales**: Letter grades, percentage scores, competency-based
- **Grade Book Management**: Recording and aggregating scores
- **Progress Reports**: Communicating learning progress
- **Standards-Based Grading**: Assessing specific competencies
- **Transcripts**: Formal academic records

## EdTech Industry Landscape

### Market Segments
- **K-12**: Classroom technology, assessment, learning platforms
- **Higher Education**: Campus management, online degrees, research tools
- **Corporate Learning**: Employee training and development
- **Test Prep**: SAT, ACT, GRE, professional certifications
- **Language Learning**: Duolingo, Babbel, immersive platforms

### Key Players
- **Content Providers**: McGraw-Hill, Cengage, Pearson, others
- **Platform Providers**: Canvas, Blackboard, Moodle communities
- **Specialized Tools**: Chegg (textbooks), Coursera (MOOCs), Udemy (courses)
- **EdTech Startups**: Innovation in vertical-specific solutions
- **Government**: Department of Education, state/local education agencies

### Emerging Trends
- **AI and Personalization**: Adaptive learning and intelligent tutoring
- **Mobile Learning**: On-demand, smartphone-based learning
- **MOOCs**: Massive Open Online Courses (Coursera, edX, etc.)
- **Micro-credentials**: Badges, certificates, micro-degrees
- **Hybrid Learning**: Blending in-person and online instruction
- **Learning Analytics**: Data-driven insights into learning and engagement
- **Open Education**: Open Educational Resources (OER) and open source tools

## Learning Accessibility

### Accessibility Standards
- **WCAG 2.1**: Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (AA/AAA levels)
- **ADA**: Americans with Disabilities Act (US legal requirement)
- **Section 504**: Rehabilitation Act (US education requirement)
- **Inclusive Design**: Designing for diverse needs from the start

### Common Accessibility Features
- **Closed Captions**: Text representation of audio
- **Screen Reader Support**: Accessible to visually impaired users
- **Keyboard Navigation**: All functions accessible via keyboard
- **Transcripts**: Text versions of video and audio content
- **High Contrast**: Sufficient color contrast ratios
- **Alt Text**: Descriptions of images and visualizations
