# Financial Technology (FinTech)

## Financial Regulations and Compliance

### Key Regulatory Frameworks

#### KYC (Know Your Customer)
- **Purpose**: Verify customer identity and assess risk
- **Requirements**: Document collection, identity verification, risk classification
- **Common Documents**: Government ID, proof of address, beneficial ownership info
- **Challenges**: Privacy concerns, identity theft risks, cost of verification

#### AML (Anti-Money Laundering)
- **Purpose**: Detect and prevent money laundering activities
- **Reporting Requirements**: File Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs)
- **Monitoring**: Ongoing transaction surveillance and pattern detection
- **Record Keeping**: Maintain detailed transaction records
- **Jurisdictions**: Requirements vary significantly by country

#### GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)
- **Scope**: Applies to companies processing personal data of EU residents
- **Key Rights**: Data subject access, erasure, portability, rectification
- **Obligations**: Consent, privacy policies, data breach notification
- **Penalties**: Up to 4% of global revenue
- **Related**: CCPA (US), similar laws in other jurisdictions

#### PCI DSS (Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard)
- **Scope**: Applies to companies handling credit card data
- **Levels**: Compliance levels based on transaction volume
- **Requirements**: Network security, access controls, testing and monitoring
- **Assessment**: Annual audits and certifications required

### Industry-Specific Regulations
- **Banking**: Bank Secrecy Act, Glass-Steagall, Dodd-Frank (US)
- **Lending**: Truth in Lending Act (TILA), Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA)
- **Securities**: Securities Act, Exchange Act, investment advisor regulations
- **Cryptocurrencies**: Evolving landscape with increasing regulation
- **Insurance**: State insurance departments, National Association of Insurance Commissioners

## Banking Systems and Payment Infrastructure

### Payment Methods and Flows

#### Card Payments
- **Credit Cards**: Revolving credit, cardholder protection
- **Debit Cards**: Direct bank account debit
- **Processing Flow**: Authorization → Clearing → Settlement
- **Networks**: Visa, Mastercard, American Express, Discover
- **Interchange Fees**: Merchant fees for card processing

#### ACH (Automated Clearing House)
- **Use Cases**: Direct deposit, bill payments, B2B transfers
- **Speed**: 1-2 business days (compared to 3+ for checks)
- **Limits**: Daily and monthly transaction limits
- **Costs**: Lower than wire transfers
- **Reversals**: Time window for reversing ACH transactions

#### Wire Transfers
- **Speed**: Same-day settlement
- **Cost**: Higher fees than ACH
- **Irrevocability**: Generally cannot be reversed
- **Tracking**: SWIFT codes, IBAN, other identifiers
- **Risks**: Higher fraud risk, requires careful verification

#### Real-Time Payment Systems
- **FedNow**: US Federal Reserve system for instant payments
- **RTP**: Real-Time Payments network
- **International**: Different systems by country (Faster Payments UK, etc.)
- **Benefits**: Immediate availability of funds
- **Challenges**: Fraud risk, limited penetration

### Banking Infrastructure
- **Core Banking Systems**: Legacy (COBOL-based) and modern cloud platforms
- **Payment Processors**: Third-party companies handling payment processing
- **Settlement Networks**: FEDWIRE, CHIPS, SWIFT for interbank transfers
- **Clearing Houses**: ACH clearing house operations
- **API Gateways**: Modern payment APIs (Stripe, Square, etc.)

## Cryptocurrency and Blockchain

### Fundamental Concepts
- **Blockchain**: Distributed ledger technology with cryptographic security
- **Bitcoin**: First cryptocurrency, proof-of-work consensus
- **Ethereum**: Blockchain with smart contract capability
- **Tokenization**: Representing assets as blockchain tokens
- **Smart Contracts**: Self-executing code on blockchain

### Cryptocurrency Types
- **Bitcoin (BTC)**: Store of value, limited supply
- **Ethereum (ETH)**: Smart contract platform, evolving use cases
- **Stablecoins**: Cryptocurrencies pegged to fiat currency (USDC, USDT, etc.)
- **Altcoins**: Alternative cryptocurrencies with varying use cases
- **DeFi Tokens**: Governance and utility tokens for decentralized finance

### DeFi (Decentralized Finance)
- **Lending/Borrowing**: Non-custodial lending protocols
- **AMM (Automated Market Makers)**: Decentralized exchanges
- **Yield Farming**: Earning returns on crypto holdings
- **Risks**: Smart contract risk, impermanent loss, liquidation risk
- **Regulation**: Increasing regulatory scrutiny

## Risk Management and Security

### Financial Risks
- **Counterparty Risk**: Risk of default by transaction partner
- **Liquidity Risk**: Inability to convert assets to cash quickly
- **Market Risk**: Price volatility impacting portfolio value
- **Operational Risk**: Failures in systems or processes
- **Compliance Risk**: Regulatory violations and penalties

### Security Practices
- **Encryption**: In-transit and at-rest data protection
- **Authentication**: Multi-factor authentication for accounts
- **Authorization**: Role-based access control
- **Audit Logging**: Comprehensive logging of all transactions
- **Fraud Detection**: Machine learning and rule-based systems
- **Key Management**: Hardware security modules (HSMs) for sensitive keys

### Incident Response
- **Breach Protocol**: Detection, investigation, notification procedures
- **Fraud Response**: Quick fund recovery, account lock procedures
- **Business Continuity**: Backup systems and disaster recovery
- **Insurance**: Cyber insurance and crime insurance policies

## Market Structure and Trading

### Market Segments
- **Equities**: Stocks and stock exchanges
- **Fixed Income**: Bonds, government securities, corporate debt
- **Foreign Exchange**: Currency trading and exchange rates
- **Commodities**: Oil, metals, agricultural products
- **Derivatives**: Options, futures, swaps

### Trading Mechanics
- **Market Orders**: Buy/sell at current market price
- **Limit Orders**: Buy/sell at specific price or better
- **High-Frequency Trading**: Algorithm-driven, microsecond-level trading
- **Circuit Breakers**: Automatic trading halts for extreme movements
- **Settlement**: T+2 or T+1 (depending on asset class)

### Market Participants
- **Brokers**: Facilitate trades for retail/institutional clients
- **Investment Banks**: Large financial institutions
- **Hedge Funds**: Alternative investment managers
- **Exchanges**: Venues for trading
- **Regulators**: SEC, FINRA, CFTC (US)

## FinTech Landscape

### Key Verticals
- **Payments**: Stripe, Square, PayPal modernizing payment processing
- **Lending**: LendingClub, Affirm, other platforms
- **Investing**: Robinhood, Wealthfront, robo-advisor platforms
- **Insurance**: Lemonade, digital-native insurance companies
- **Banking**: Chime, Revolut, digital banks

### Emerging Trends
- **Open Banking**: APIs enabling third-party integrations
- **Embedded Finance**: Financial services within non-financial apps
- **Super Apps**: Platforms combining multiple financial services
- **Blockchain/Crypto**: Integration into traditional finance
- **Data and Analytics**: AI/ML for personalization and risk assessment
